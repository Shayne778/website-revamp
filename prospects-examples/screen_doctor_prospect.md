- Existing service categories
- Existing project photos
- Existing testimonials

- Existing service areas
- Existing phone number and contact form structure

### Do not invent

- New reviews
- New awards
- New certifications
- New guarantees
- New financing offers
- New emergency services
- New service areas
- New before-and-after claims
- New “number one” or “best” claims

### Be careful with

- Better Business Bureau wording: the public profile says A+ rating but not accredited.
- Timeline wording: the current site includes estimate and completion timelines. These may change, so either omit them from the mockup or mark them as “current schedule to be confirmed.”
- Award wording: use only award years clearly listed.
- “Since 1995” wording: the current website uses this language, but public business start dates may differ. Use the site’s phrasing or keep it as “serving homeowners since 1995” rather than “founded in 1995.”
