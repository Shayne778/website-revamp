# Project Brief: Local Business Website Transformation Service

## Project Summary

This business identifies local small businesses with outdated or underperforming websites, extracts the useful existing business information and assets from those websites, and transforms them into modern website concepts.

The goal is not to randomly redesign websites from scratch. The goal is to take an existing local business website that already contains valuable material — such as service descriptions, photos, branding, contact information, credentials, and business history — and convert that material into a cleaner, more professional, mobile-first website mockup.

The upgraded website concept is then shown privately to the business owner as a sales asset, with the goal of converting them into a paying client.

## Core Business Model

1. Find local businesses with weak or outdated websites.
2. Confirm that the business itself appears reputable and active.
3. Scrape or manually extract useful information from the current website.
4. Download usable public-facing assets from the old website.
5. Organize the extracted material into a structured redesign brief.
6. Build a modern homepage or small website concept using the business's real information and assets.
7. Send the owner a private concept mockup as outreach.
8. Offer to turn the mockup into a completed website refresh.

## Key Principle

The best prospect is not simply a business with a bad website.

The best prospect is a good local business whose current website makes the business look worse than it actually is.

Ideal businesses already have useful raw material on their site, but the material is poorly presented.

Examples:

- Good service descriptions buried in cluttered pages
- Real project photos displayed in a dated gallery
- Strong reputation but weak homepage messaging
- Local service expertise hidden behind poor design
- Useful business history written in long, unstructured paragraphs
- Contact information present but not conversion-focused

## Best Initial Niches

### Highest Priority: Home Services

Home services are likely the strongest starting category because one new customer can be valuable, many local websites are outdated, owners understand calls and quote requests, and the websites are usually simple enough to rebuild quickly.

Strong home-service niches:

- Pool service
- Pool cage repair
- Roofing
- Heating, ventilation, and air conditioning
- Plumbing
- Pest control
- Landscaping and hardscaping
- Hurricane windows and shutters
- Flooring
- Remodeling
- Painting
- Tree service
- Boat repair and marine services

### Secondary Priority: Private-Pay Health, Wellness, and Aesthetics

These businesses can be strong targets because trust, presentation, and professionalism matter.

Examples:

- Med spas
- Cosmetic dentists
- Chiropractic clinics
- Physical therapy clinics
- Veterinary clinics
- Private-pay wellness clinics

Caution: For health-related businesses, do not add unsupported medical claims, fake testimonials, fake before-and-after images, or language implying guaranteed outcomes.

## Prospect Selection Criteria

Prioritize businesses with:

- Google rating of 4.4 stars or higher
- At least 20 reviews
- A visible current website
- An outdated or weak mobile experience
- Useful existing content on the old site
- Real photos or gallery assets
- Clear services with commercial value
- Weak or unclear calls to action
- Owner-operated or locally managed feel
- Recent signs of business activity

Avoid businesses with:

- Already polished websites
- Franchise or national-brand websites
- Ecommerce-heavy websites
- Complex portals, logins, booking systems, or patient portals
- No usable public-facing content
- No signs of recent business activity
- Highly regulated content that requires specialized compliance knowledge

## Website Transformation Scope

The initial deliverable is a private concept mockup, not a full production website.

Recommended first mockup:

- One-page homepage redesign
- Mobile-first layout
- Modern hero section
- Clear service sections
- About or trust section
- Gallery or project-photo section if assets are available
- Review or reputation section only if real reviews are available from legitimate public sources
- Service-area section
- Contact section
- Quote-request form layout
- Strong call-to-action buttons

## Scraping and Asset Intake Workflow

The existing website is the source material for the redesign.

The purpose of scraping is to preserve what is already valuable while improving presentation, structure, and conversion flow.

### Information to Extract

- Business name
- Logo
- Tagline or slogan
- Phone number
- Email address
- Physical address
- Hours
- Service areas
- Services offered
- About/company history
- Team information
- Certifications
- Licenses
- Awards
- Warranties or guarantees, if clearly stated
- Existing testimonials published on the business site
- Calls to action
- Existing page structure
- Frequently repeated keywords
- Important local search phrases

### Assets to Download

- Logo files
- Homepage images
- Gallery photos
- Project photos
- Team photos
- Service photos
- Icons, if useful
- Brochures or PDFs
- Before-and-after photos, only if already publicly used by the business

### Information to Avoid Inventing

Do not fabricate:

- Reviews
- Awards
- Certifications
- Licenses
- Guarantees
- Years in business
- Service areas
- Medical or technical claims
- Before-and-after outcomes
- Team members
- Partnerships

If something is unclear, use neutral placeholder language or mark it as needing owner confirmation.

## Ethical and Legal Positioning

The mockup should be private and clearly presented as a concept.

Do not publish the concept publicly in a way that implies affiliation with the business.

Do not imply that the business approved, commissioned, or owns the redesign unless they have explicitly agreed.

Safer positioning:

> I made a private concept mockup showing how your current website could look with a cleaner layout, stronger mobile experience, and clearer quote-request flow.

Avoid saying or implying:

- We are your web design provider.
- This is your new official website.
- Your website is bad.
- We used your materials because they are free for anyone to reuse.

## Outreach Positioning

Do not insult the business owner or frame the pitch as criticism.

Bad angle:

> Your website is outdated and needs work.

Better angle:

> You already seem to have a reputable local business, but your current website may not be showing that as clearly as it could, especially on mobile.

Best angle:

> I used the information already available on your current site to create a private concept showing how your business could look with a cleaner layout, stronger service sections, and a clearer quote-request flow.

## Practical Workflow

1. Pick one niche.
2. Find 50 businesses in the target region.
3. Score each prospect.
4. Choose the top 10.
5. Scrape the current website for content and assets.
6. Create an intake file for each business.
7. Build 3 to 5 homepage mockups.
8. Send personalized outreach.
9. Track responses.
10. Refine the niche, offer, and mockup style based on results.
