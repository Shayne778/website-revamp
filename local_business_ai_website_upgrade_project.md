# Local Business AI Website Upgrade Project

## Project Summary

The business idea is to identify local small businesses with outdated or underperforming websites, create an upgraded AI-assisted website concept, and show the improved version to the business owner as a sample in hopes of converting them into a paying client.

The goal is not to randomly redesign websites. The goal is to target businesses where a better website can reasonably improve trust, lead generation, calls, quote requests, bookings, or perceived professionalism.

---

## Core Strategy

Do **not** choose targets mainly based on estimated website traffic.

Most small local-business websites have low or poorly measured traffic, so tools like Ahrefs, Semrush, Similarweb, and Ubersuggest may be inaccurate or show little data. Traffic can be used as a weak signal, but it should not drive the decision.

The better target is:

> A business where one new customer is valuable, customers search online before buying, the current website is weak, and the website is simple enough to rebuild quickly.

## Prospect Scoring System

Score each business from 0 to 3 in each category.

| Factor | What to Look For | Why It Matters |
|---|---|---|
| Website quality gap | Dated design, bad mobile layout, tiny text, clutter, weak visuals | Easy visual before-and-after improvement |
| Business value | One customer is worth hundreds or thousands of dollars | Easier to justify paying for a better website |
| Search dependence | Customers likely Google this business before buying | Better web presence can generate leads |
| Site simplicity | 5 to 8 pages, no ecommerce, no portal, no complex tools | Faster and easier to rebuild |
| Trust gap | Good Google reviews but poor website | Website underrepresents the business quality |
| Owner accessibility | Owner or local manager is easy to identify | Easier outreach and sales conversation |

### Score Interpretation

| Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0 to 8 | Weak target |
| 9 to 12 | Possible target |
| 13 to 15 | Strong target |
| 16 to 18 | Excellent target |

Ideal prospect:

> A local owner-operated business with strong reviews, a weak website, high-value services, simple site structure, and clear dependence on local search.

## Specific Target Criteria

Prioritize businesses with:

- Google rating of 4.4 stars or higher
- At least 20 reviews
- A visible website
- An outdated or weak mobile experience
- No clear call-to-action
- No quote form or poor contact flow
- Strong service value
- Owner-operated or locally managed structure
- Recent Google reviews showing the business is active

Avoid businesses with:

- Very modern websites
- National/franchise branding
- Complex website systems
- Ecommerce stores
- Legal or heavily regulated content unless you understand the niche
- No signs of recent activity

## How to Estimate Demand

Traffic tools can help, but they are imperfect for small businesses.

Useful tools:

- Ahrefs
- Semrush
- Similarweb
- Ubersuggest
- Google Keyword Planner
- Keywords Everywhere

Use them to estimate:

- Organic traffic
- Keywords the site ranks for
- Search demand for the service
- Competitor visibility
- Commercial intent

Do not disqualify a business just because a traffic tool shows little or no traffic. Many small local sites do not have enough measurable data.

Better demand signals:

- People search for the service locally.
- Competitors have strong websites.
- The business has strong reviews but weak web presence.
- The business sells high-value services.
- The Google Business Profile is active.
- The current site has poor mobile usability or weak conversion flow.

## Website Upgrade Scope

The sample should be a modernized concept, not a full production site.

Recommended concept build:

- Homepage
- Services section
- About section
- Review or reputation section
- Gallery section, if appropriate
- Contact section
- Quote request form
- Strong call-to-action buttons
- Mobile-first layout

Keep the concept simple and visually impressive.

Do not:

- Pretend the concept is the company’s actual website
- Use fake reviews
- Use fake before-and-after images
- Make unsupported claims
- Use their logo in a way that suggests you represent them
- Publish the concept publicly in a misleading way

Safer positioning:

> “I made a private concept mockup showing how your current website could look with a cleaner layout, stronger mobile experience, and clearer quote-request flow.”

## Outreach Positioning

Avoid insulting the business owner.

Do not say:

> “Your website is bad.”

Better angles:

> “You already have strong reviews, but your website does not reflect the quality of the business as well as it could.”

> “I made a quick concept showing how your site could look with a cleaner mobile layout, stronger service sections, and clearer quote-request flow.”

> “No obligation. I figured it may be useful to see what a refreshed version could look like.”

The strongest pitch is:

> Your business appears to be good, but your website may be underrepresenting it.

## Practical Workflow

### Step 1: Pick One Niche

Start narrow.

Example:

> Pool service and pool cage repair businesses in Fort Myers, Cape Coral, Naples, Bonita Springs, and Estero.

### Step 2: Build a Prospect List

Find 50 businesses using Google Maps and local directories.

### Step 3: Score Each Business

Use the scoring system to identify the strongest opportunities.

### Step 4: Choose the Top 10

Do not redesign all 50. Pick the highest-scoring prospects.

### Step 5: Build 3 to 5 Mockups

Create polished, simple, one-page redesign concepts.

### Step 6: Send Personalized Outreach

Contact owners by email, phone, contact form, or direct message.

### Step 7: Track Response Rate

Measure:

- Open rate, if available
- Reply rate
- Positive reply rate
- Calls booked
- Sales closed
- Average sale price

### Step 8: Repeat or Adjust

If one niche does not respond, test another.

Possible next niches:

- Roofing
- Pool service
- Landscaping
- Pest control
- Med spas
- Veterinary clinics
- Hurricane window companies

## Key Principle

The best prospects are not necessarily the businesses with the most website traffic.

The best prospects are businesses where:

1. The current website clearly looks weak.
2. The business itself appears reputable.
3. A single new customer is valuable.
4. The business relies on local search and trust.
5. The site is simple enough to upgrade quickly.
6. The owner can understand the value immediately.

In simple terms:

> Find good local businesses whose websites make them look worse than they are.
