# Prospects: Local Business Website Transformation Pipeline

## Purpose

This file tracks businesses that may be good candidates for a website transformation.

A good prospect is a reputable local business with an outdated or underperforming website that still contains useful business information, service details, photos, branding, and other assets that can be reused as source material for a modern redesign concept.

The goal is to identify businesses where a redesigned website could improve trust, professionalism, lead generation, calls, quote requests, or bookings.

## Prospect Scoring System

Score each category from 0 to 3.

| Factor | Score 0 | Score 1 | Score 2 | Score 3 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Website quality gap | Already modern | Slightly dated | Clearly dated | Very outdated or poor mobile experience |
| Business value | Low-value transactions | Moderate value | High value | One customer can be worth hundreds or thousands |
| Search dependence | Mostly referral/walk-in | Some online search | Strong online research behavior | Customers clearly compare providers online |
| Site simplicity | Complex site | Some complexity | Mostly simple | Simple brochure-style site |
| Trust gap | Website matches business quality | Minor mismatch | Website underrepresents business | Strong reviews but weak website |
| Asset availability | Little usable content | Some text only | Useful text and some images | Strong raw material: photos, services, branding |
| Owner accessibility | No clear contact path | Generic contact only | Local manager/contact likely | Owner-operated feel or clear decision-maker |

## Score Interpretation

| Total Score | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 0 to 9 | Weak target |
| 10 to 14 | Possible target |
| 15 to 18 | Strong target |
| 19 to 21 | Excellent target |

## Ideal Prospect Profile

The ideal prospect has:

- A weak or outdated current website
- Useful existing content on the site
- Real photos or gallery images
- Clear service descriptions
- Strong Google reviews
- High-value services
- A simple website structure
- Local search dependence
- An obvious quote, call, appointment, or booking goal
- Owner-operated or local-business feel

In plain terms:

> Find good businesses whose websites make them look worse than they are.
